What is a candlestick chart?

Candlestick charts are used to depict price movements within a specific time frame (in this case, one day). The main part, or "real body," indicates the range between the opening and closing prices:

  • Green body signifies a price increase
  • Red body indicates a price decrease

Shadows (or wicks) extend from the body to show the highest and lowest prices reached during the day.

Technical Analysis Terms

Resistance Level: A price point where selling pressure prevents further price increases.

Support Level: A price point where buying interest prevents further price decreases.

Trendline: A line drawn over pivot highs or under pivot lows to help visualize the direction of price movement.
